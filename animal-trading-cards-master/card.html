Rhesus Monkey

Rhesus monkeys are the most common nonhuman primates used in biomedical research.

  • Family: Cercopithecidae
  • Mass: 12-17 lbs
  • Gestation period: 166 days
  • Class: Mammalia

The rhesus monkey, is a species of Old World monkey. There are between six and nine recognised subspecies that are split between two groups, the Chinese-derived and the Indian-derived.
